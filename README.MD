# MedTech Sales Capacity Optimization

Business Process Analysis & Digital Transformation Case Study : 
# 1. Executive Summary

A MedTech equipment distributor operating in Kanpur Uttar Pradesh, sells physiotherapy and rehabilitation devices to doctors, physiotherapists, sports teams, and clinics.

Despite strong market demand and annual revenue of approximately ₹5 crore, the company faces a major operational constraint: limited demonstration capacity.

Medical equipment sales require product demonstrations, but the owner can only visit a limited number of clients.

As a result, many potential customers purchase from competitors.


### *_This case study analyzes the current workflow and proposes a digital transformation strategy to scale sales operations._*

# 2. Business Context

#### _The company sells equipment used in:_

- Physiotherapy clinics
- Rehabilitation centers
- Sports recovery facilities

_Example products include:_

- Shoulder wheel devices
- Quadriceps rehabilitation tables
- Parallel walking bars
- Hand exercise boards
- Suspension therapy systems

Customers typically require product demonstrations before purchasing, making demonstrations the most critical step in the sales process.

# 3. Problem Statement


_The company’s sales model depends heavily on in-person demonstrations._

_This creates a capacity bottleneck._

#### Example Sales Scenario

| Metric | Value |
|------|------|
| Potential customers | 20 |
| Demonstrations completed | 10 |
| Missed opportunities | 10 |

Missed demonstrations directly lead to lost revenue.

# 4. Root Cause Analysis

The operational bottleneck comes from three structural limitations.

1. **Demonstration Dependency**

Medical devices require explanation and physical interaction before purchase.

2. **Limited Sales Bandwidth**

_The owner personally manages most demonstrations._

3. **Lack of Digital Infrastructure**

The company currently has:

- No website  
- No digital product catalog  
- No CRM system  

Customer records are stored manually.

# 5. Key Insight

The company does not have a demand problem.

It has a **demonstration scalability problem**.

If demonstrations can be scaled, revenue can increase without expanding the sales team significantly.

# 6. Business Requirements Document (BRD)

## Business Requirements

### BR-1 Lead Management
The system should store all potential customers including:
- Name
- Clinic
- Contact
- Product interest
- Location

### BR-2 Product Demonstration
The system should allow:
- Video demonstrations
- Online meeting scheduling
- Product documentation access

### BR-3 Order Tracking
The system should record:
- Product orders
- Customer details
- Delivery status

---
# 7. Proposed Solution

### 1. Digital Product Demonstrations

Create professional **video demonstrations of medical equipment**.

**Benefits:**

- Doctors can evaluate products remotely
- Reduces need for physical visits
- Enables multiple demonstrations per day

---

### 2. Product Catalog Website

**A website containing:**

- Product information
- Demonstration videos
- Technical specifications
- Inquiry form

**Benefits:**

- Increases brand visibility
- Generates inbound leads

---

### 3. CRM System

**Implement a CRM system to manage:**

- Customer contacts
- Lead tracking
- Follow-ups
- Demonstration scheduling

**Possible tools:**

- Odoo
- Zoho CRM
- HubSpot CRM

---

### 4. Hybrid Demonstration Model

_Introduce online demonstrations using platforms like:_

- Zoom
- Google Meet

_This reduces travel time and increases reach._

## 8. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### KPI 1 — Sales Capacity

### KPI 2 — Lead Conversion Rate

**Before**
