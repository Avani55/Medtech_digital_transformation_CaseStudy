# MedTech Business Process Analysis & Digital Transformation Proposal

## Project Overview

This case study analyzes the traditional sales process of a **MedTech equipment distributor** and proposes a **digital transformation strategy** to improve sales capacity and operational efficiency.

The company manufactures and sells **physiotherapy, rehabilitation, and medical modality equipment** to healthcare professionals and sports organizations.

The study focuses on identifying **operational bottlenecks** and proposing **scalable digital solutions**.

---

# Industry Context

The MedTech company operates in the **physiotherapy and rehabilitation equipment market** and sells products such as:

- Shoulder wheels  
- Quadriceps tables  
- Parallel walking bars  
- Hand exercise boards  
- Suspension therapy equipment  

### Target Customers

- Physiotherapists  
- Doctors  
- Sports teams  
- Clinics  
- Hospitals  
- Athletes  

The company currently operates under a **regional medical sales license in Uttar Pradesh (India).**

---

# Current Business Model (Traditional Sales Model)

The company follows a **direct demonstration-based B2B sales process.**

### Step 1: Lead Identification

Potential customers include **doctors, physiotherapists, sports teams, and clinics.**

### Step 2: Physical Visit

The owner personally visits the customer.

### Step 3: Product Demonstration

Medical devices require **physical demonstration and explanation before purchase.**

### Step 4: Order Placement

If satisfied, the customer places an order.

### Step 5: Product Delivery

Equipment is supplied to the **clinic or medical facility.**

---

# Key Business Problem

## Sales Capacity Bottleneck

The business heavily depends on **physical demonstrations**, which limits how many clients can be served.

### Example Scenario

| Metric | Value |
|------|------|
| Total potential clients | 20 |
| Demonstrations possible | 10 |
| Lost opportunities | 10 |

Because doctors rarely buy medical devices **without seeing them**, missed demonstrations result in **lost revenue**.

---

# Additional Operational Challenges

During discussions with the business owner, several **inefficiencies** were identified.

---

## 1. No Centralized Customer Database

Customer records are stored **informally on a laptop.**

### Risks

- Data loss  
- No structured tracking  
- Difficult customer follow-ups  

---

## 2. No Digital Presence

The company has:

- No website  
- No product catalog online  
- No digital marketing  

This limits **brand visibility.**

---

## 3. Sales Team Retention Problem

The owner faces difficulty hiring salespeople because:

- Once trained, employees often start their own business  
- Some join competitors  

This creates **knowledge leakage and instability in the sales team.**

---

# Business Analysis (Root Cause)

The **primary bottleneck** is:

## Demonstration Dependency

Medical equipment sales require:

- Trust  
- Hands-on explanation  
- Clinical understanding  

Therefore, **sales growth is limited by the number of physical demonstrations per day.**

---

# Proposed Digital Transformation Strategy

## 1. Product Demonstration Videos

Create **professional demonstration videos** for each medical device.

### Benefits

- Doctors can view the product remotely  
- Reduces need for physical visits  
- Scales demonstrations to unlimited customers  

---

## 2. MedTech Product Website

Develop a **website** containing:

- Product catalog  
- Demonstration videos  
- Equipment specifications  
- Online inquiry form  

### Benefits

- Online visibility  
- Lead generation  
- Trust building  

---

## 3. CRM System for Lead Management

Implement a **CRM system** to track:

- Doctor contacts  
- Follow-ups  
- Orders  
- Demonstration schedules  

### Possible Tools

- Custom CRM  
- Odoo  
- Zoho CRM  

### Benefits

- Organized customer management  
- Sales tracking  
- Follow-up automation  

---

## 4. Online Demonstration Model

Instead of only **physical visits**, offer **video demonstrations via Zoom / Google Meet.**

### Benefits

- More doctors can be reached per day  
- Reduces travel time  
- Improves sales conversion  

---

# Expected Business Impact

| Problem | Proposed Solution | Impact |
|------|------|------|
| Limited demonstrations | Video demos + online meetings | Higher sales capacity |
| No digital presence | Website + product catalog | More leads |
| Poor data management | CRM system | Organized sales tracking |
| Dependence on physical visits | Remote demos | Scalable sales |
